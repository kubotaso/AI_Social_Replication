Dataset columns you need to reproduce **Figure 1** (1993 GSS culture module):

## 1) Filter / identifiers
- `YEAR` (to restrict to **1993**)
- `ID` (not required for calculations, but useful for tracking respondents / merges)

## 2) Control variable (used in every logit + for the “sample mean education” reference line)
- `EDUC` (years of education; continuous)

## 3) The 18 music-genre taste items (5-point like/dislike + DK/NA)
These are the core inputs for:
- each genre’s **dislike DV** (`dislike it` or `dislike very much`)
- the **tolerance count** (number of other genres *not* disliked)
- the **genre-audience education mean** (mean `EDUC` among those who “like” a genre)

Exact columns (as shown in your documentation):
1. `LATIN` (Latin/Mariachi/Salsa)
2. `JAZZ`
3. `BLUES` (Blues or Rhythm and Blues)
4. `MUSICALS` (Broadway musicals/show tunes)
5. `OLDIES` (Oldies rock)
6. `CLASSICL` (Classical—symphony & chamber)
7. `BIGBAND` (Big Band/Swing)
8. `NEWAGE` (New age/space music)
9. `OPERA`
10. `BLUGRASS` (Bluegrass)
11. `FOLK`
12. `REGGAE`
13. `MOODEASY` (Mood/easy listening)
14. `CONROCK` (Contemporary pop/rock)
15. `RAP`
16. `HVYMETAL` (Heavy metal)
17. `COUNTRY` (Country/Western)
18. `GOSPEL`

## Robust fallbacks if your extract uses different names
If any of the above are missing in your file, look for the same 1993 music battery under alternate/legacy names like:
- `MUSICLAT`, `MUSICJAZ`, `MUSICBLU`, `MUSICMUS`, `MUSICOLD`, `MUSICCLA`, `MUSICBIG`, `MUSICNEW`, `MUSICOPR`, `MUSICBLG`, `MUSICFOL`, `MUSICREG`, `MUSICEZL`, `MUSICPOP`/`MUSICROK`, `MUSICRAP`, `MUSICMET`, `MUSICCNT`, `MUSICGOS`
or “LIKE/DISLIKE” prefixes such as `LIKEJAZZ`, `LIKEOPERA`, etc. (same response scale).

Those 20 variables (`YEAR`, `ID`, `EDUC`, and the 18 genre items) are sufficient to compute every element plotted in Figure 1.
