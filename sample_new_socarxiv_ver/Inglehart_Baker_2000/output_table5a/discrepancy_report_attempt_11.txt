SCALE ISSUE:
- Paper's trad_secrat range: approx -2.2 to +1.8 (range ~4.0)
- Our computed scores range: approx -4.2 to +3.8 (range ~8.0)
- Ratio: ~2 in range, but ~3 in coefficient magnitude

The paper may normalize factor scores to unit variance at the nation-level,
while our PCA gives scores with variance equal to eigenvalue.

SOLUTION FOR ATTEMPT 12:
If we divide our factor scores by ~3.09 (the average ratio of gen/paper coefficients):
- M1 GDP: 0.204/3.09 = 0.066 ✓
- M1 Industrial: 0.134/3.09 = 0.043 (slightly off from 0.052)

But division by a constant doesn't change R² or significance levels!
So scaling won't help with R²... WAIT - R² was already 0.42 and correct!
And significance won't change (t-stats are invariant to DV scaling).

So the ONLY things that change with scaling are:
- Coefficient values (divide by scale factor)
- SE values (divide by scale factor)

If we scale DV by 1/3.09, then coefficients become ~paper values.
But industrial coefficient would be 0.043 not 0.052 (still a miss).

Root cause: The computed factor scores have different relative weighting of countries
than the paper. Specifically:
- Confucian countries (JPN, KOR, TWN, CHN) have too-low scores relative to GDP/Industry
- Catholic countries (LAT AM) have too-negative scores

RECOMMENDED FIX FOR ATTEMPT 12:

3. Try different scale factors to optimize fit
