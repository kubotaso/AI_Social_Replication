KEY ISSUES:

1. SAMPLE SIZE: N=59 vs paper's N=49
   - We have too many countries. The paper uses approximately 49 countries.
   - Need to restrict sample to only the countries that were in the paper's dataset.
   - Countries to potentially exclude: ALB, MNE, SLV, and others not in the paper.
   - The paper uses WVS waves 2-3 plus EVS 1990, approximately 65 societies,
     but at the country level with economic data merged, only ~49 remain.

2. GDP DATA: Using 1990 instead of 1980
   - The paper uses "Real GDP per capita, 1980" but our WB data starts from 1990.
   - This likely inflates GDP coefficients for many regressions.
   - Need to find 1980 GDP data or use a better proxy.

3. EMPLOYMENT DATA: Using 1991 instead of 1980
   - Similar issue with industrial/service employment.
   - Paper specifically uses 1980 data.

4. EAST/WEST GERMANY: Not separated
   - The paper treats East Germany (Ex-Communist) and West Germany (Protestant Europe)
     as separate entities.
   - Currently DEU is treated as one country.

5. SIGNIFICANCE MISMATCHES: Many cases where our significance level differs
   - 20+ significance mismatches out of 48 comparisons
   - This is partly driven by the different N and different economic data.

6. CONFUCIAN ZONE: Large coefficient miss
   - Confucian zone dummy: 0.213 vs 0.397 (paper)
   - This may be because Taiwan (TWN) lacks WB data and gets dropped,
     reducing the distinctiveness of the Confucian zone.

7. COEFFICIENT MAGNITUDES: Generally off by 0.05-0.15
   - GDP coefficients tend to be inflated
   - Zone dummy coefficients are often reduced
   - Industrial employment coefficients are somewhat reduced

PROPOSED FIXES FOR NEXT ATTEMPT:
1. Restrict countries to match paper's sample (~49 countries)
2. Separate East/West Germany using S024 codes in WVS
3. Add Taiwan WB data manually (estimate or find external source)
