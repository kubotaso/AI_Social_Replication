Figure 2: Economic Zones for 65 Societies Superimposed on Two Dimensions of Cross-Cultural Variation

Type: Same scatter plot as Figure 1 but WITHOUT country labels (or minimal labels).
Instead, economic zone boundaries are superimposed showing 4 GNP per capita zones:
1. "Less than $2,000 GNP per capita" - lower-left zone
2. "$2,000 to $5,000 GNP per capita" - upper-left zone
3. "$5,000 to $15,000 GNP per capita" - center zone
4. "More than $15,000 GNP per capita" - upper-right zone

The boundaries between economic zones are solid curved lines.
All data points from Figure 1 are shown.

X-axis: Survival/Self-Expression Dimension (-2.0 to 2.0)
Y-axis: Traditional/Secular-Rational Dimension (-2.2 to 1.8)

Note: All but one of the 65 societies fit into the economic zones; only the Dominican Republic is mislocated.
Source: GNP per capita is based on the World Bank's Purchasing Power Parity estimates as of 1995, in U.S. dollars (World Bank 1997:214-15).

The 4 zones correspond to:
- 19 societies with GNP > $15,000: upper-right corner
- Societies with $5,000-$15,000: middle band
- Societies with $2,000-$5,000: lower band
- Societies with < $2,000: lower-left corner
