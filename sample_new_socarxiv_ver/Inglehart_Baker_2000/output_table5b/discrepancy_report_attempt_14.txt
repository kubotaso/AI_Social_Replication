KEY INSIGHT ON COLLINEARITY:
The fundamental problem is that in our data:
- Orthodox countries = {BGR, MKD, ROU, RUS, SRB, UKR} all have low surv_selfexp scores
- Ex-Communist countries = {BGR, CHN, CZE, EST, HUN, LVA, MKD, POL, ROU, RUS, SRB, SVK, UKR}
  - The overlap: BGR, MKD, ROU, RUS, SRB, UKR are both Communist AND Orthodox
  - Only EST, LVA are Communist-and-NOT-Orthodox (they're also Protestant)
  - Only CHN, CZE, HUN, POL, SVK are Communist-and-NOT-Orthodox-NOT-Protestant
- In M6, when both Communist AND Orthodox dummies are in the model:
  - Communist captures the CHN, CZE, HUN, POL, SVK, EST, LVA signal
  - Orthodox should capture the ADDITIONAL Orthodox effect beyond Communist
  - But all Orthodox are also Communist -> collinearity weakens Orthodox coefficient
  - Paper apparently gets Orthodox = -1.182 which implies much larger additional effect

POSSIBLE SOLUTION:
The paper may have a different factor score for these Orthodox countries. Their surv_selfexp
scores might be much lower in the paper's original analysis. If RUS, UKR score more
negatively (e.g., -2.0 to -2.5 instead of -1.5 to -1.6), the Orthodox coefficient
would be much larger in magnitude.

1990s would be small) - but that contradicts the instruction_summary.
