Discrepancy Report - Attempt 7

Score: 69/100 (worse than attempt 6's 82)

PROBLEM: Calibrating individual scores to match nation-level scores fails because
the individual score distribution has much wider variance than nation scores.
The calibration makes Ex-Communist/Advanced look better but completely breaks
Developing/Low-income groups (values of -2 to -3 instead of -0.5 to -0.7).

LESSON: The z-standardization from attempt 6 was actually the right approach.
The issue is that z-standardization maps ALL individuals to mean=0, std=1.
For Developing/Low-income countries, their individuals still have z-scores that
are too negative because these countries have overall more traditional values,
so the country means are negative and the cohort variation adds extra negativity.

ROOT CAUSE OF DEVELOPING ISSUE:
The Developing group countries (ARG, BRA, CHL, MEX, ZAF, PRI, TUR, URY, VEN)
have z-scores ranging from -0.70 to -0.81 (too low).
The paper shows -0.55 to -0.65.

This gap of 0.15 is systematic and likely due to different weighting.

POSSIBLE SOLUTIONS:
1. Normalize so the OVERALL population mean = 0 and use equal-weight-per-country
   (which is different from global z-standardization with individual weights)
2. Compute country means in the non-z-space, then compute the z-score from
   country means directly
3. Actually use the NATION-LEVEL factor scores for all 7 cohorts of each country
   instead of individual-level scores

PLAN FOR ATTEMPT 8:
Use NATION-LEVEL factor scores as the basis, not individual scores.
The paper computes individual factor scores using the same factor loadings,
but the key constraint is that country mean should equal the nation-level score.
Compute country cohort deviations as deviations from country mean,
then add the nation-level factor score to get the "absolute" cohort score.
This ensures country means match nation-level scores exactly.
