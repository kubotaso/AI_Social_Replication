ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
The z-standardization approach gives biased results for groups with very few
countries in early cohorts. When only 1-2 countries have data for the oldest
cohort (Low-income oldest: n=12), the mean is not representative.

KEY INSIGHT for attempt 7:
Instead of z-standardizing individual scores and then computing means,
try using the RAW factor scores (not z-standardized) and see if that
gives values closer to the paper.

Alternatively, the paper may weight by INDIVIDUAL within each country-cohort,
not by country. The "equal weight for each society" instruction means:
  - Compute mean for each SOCIETY (country) in each cohort
  - Then average SOCIETIES (not individuals)
This is what we do, but maybe the equal-weighting should be across
societies differently.
