MAIN ISSUES:

1. Advanced industrial values are far too high (0.80 at youngest cohort vs paper's ~0.42)
   ROOT CAUSE: 10 of 22 advanced industrial countries come from EVS data which lacks
   age/birth year variables. The 12 available countries are biased toward highly secular
   nations (Germany, Sweden, Norway, Japan etc). Missing countries include Ireland (-2.52),
   Portugal (-1.06), Austria (-0.01), Italy (+0.34), Belgium (+0.88) which would pull
   the average down significantly.

   FIX: Use deviation-based approach: compute cohort deviations from country means using
   available countries, then add the group mean computed from ALL countries (including those
   without cohort data). This approach was tested and gives Advanced industrial values of
   -0.34 to 0.56, still higher than paper's -0.07 to 0.42 but much closer.
