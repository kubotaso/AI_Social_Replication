Figure 7: Traditional/Secular-Rational Values by Year of Birth for Four Types of Societies

Type: Line graph
X-axis: Birth Year (7 cohorts: 1907-1916, 1917-1926, 1927-1936, 1937-1946, 1947-1956, 1957-1966, 1967-1976)
Y-axis: Traditional/Secular-Rational Dimension (range approximately -1.0 to 0.6)
High values = secular-rational; low values = traditional.

Four lines, one for each society type:
1. Ex-Communist societies (solid thick line, starts around 0 and rises steeply to ~0.4)
   a = Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Bosnia, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Rep., Estonia, Georgia, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Macedonia, Moldova, Poland, Romania, Russia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Ukraine, Yugoslavia (N = 15,804)

2. Advanced industrial democracies (dashed line, starts around -0.3 and rises to ~0.3)
   b = Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Finland, France, East Germany, West Germany, Great Britain, Iceland, Ireland, Northern Ireland, Italy, Japan, South Korea, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, and the United States (N = 21,947)

3. Developing societies (dotted line, relatively flat, around -0.5 to -0.3)
   c = Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Mexico, South Africa, Puerto Rico, Turkey, Uruguay, Venezuela (N = 8,024)

4. Low-income societies (thin dashed line, relatively flat, around -0.6 to -0.8)
   d = Dominican Republic, Ghana, India, Nigeria, Peru, Philippines (N = 5,280)

Key findings:
- Steepest intergenerational differences in advanced industrial societies and ex-Communist societies
- Relatively flat slopes in developing and low-income societies
- Younger cohorts are markedly less traditional in advanced industrial and ex-Communist societies

Data: Most recent survey for each country. Data are weighted to give each society equal weight.
