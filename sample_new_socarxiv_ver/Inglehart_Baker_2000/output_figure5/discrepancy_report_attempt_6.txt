STRATEGIC ANALYSIS:
The fundamental problem is the PCA/calibration approach cannot perfectly match the paper.
The raw factor scores from our PCA differ substantially from the paper's SPSS factor scores.

The paper states it uses factor analysis with 10 items to create the cultural map (Figure 1).
Figure 5 then plots the SAME religious subgroups in the SAME factor space.

INSIGHT: The paper likely computed factor scores using the OVERALL WVS sample (including
all waves and countries), not just the nation-level means. Subgroup scores were then the
MEAN of those individual-level factor scores within each subgroup.

The best approach for attempt 7:
- Use nation-level paper coordinates (from Figure 1) directly for the national means
- For each country's religious subgroups, compute the DEVIATION from the national mean
  in the PCA space, then apply the same scale factor to the deviation to convert to paper coords
- Formula: subgroup_paper = nation_paper + scale_factor * (subgroup_pca - nation_pca)
  where scale_factor is learned from the calibration fit

This "centered" approach should give better results because:
- The national center position is fixed from paper Figure 1
- Only the within-country deviation needs estimation
- This is inherently more accurate for small differences between subgroups

ATTEMPT 7 STRATEGY:
Implement "anchored" approach:
1. Nation-level positions anchored to Figure 1 paper coordinates
2. Compute raw subgroup deviation from national mean in PCA space
3. Scale deviations to paper coordinate units using the calibration fit
4. subgroup_position = anchor_position + scaled_deviation
