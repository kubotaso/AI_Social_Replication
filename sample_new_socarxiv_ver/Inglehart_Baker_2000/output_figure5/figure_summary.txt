Figure 5: Differences between the Religious Groups within Religiously Mixed Societies on Two Dimensions of Cross-Cultural Variation

Type: Scatter plot similar to cultural map but showing WITHIN-COUNTRY religious group differences.
X-axis: Survival/Self-Expression Dimension (-2.0 to 2.0)
Y-axis: Traditional/Secular-Rational Dimension (-2.2 to 1.8)

Points represent religious subgroups within specific countries:
- West Germany: Protestant (~0.6, 1.3), Catholic (~0.4, 1.2)
- Switzerland: Protestant (~0.8, 0.5), Catholic (~0.5, 0.4)
- Netherlands: Protestant (~1.3, 0.5), Catholic (~1.0, 0.4)
- India: Hindu (~-0.3, -0.5), Muslim (~-0.5, -1.0)
- Nigeria: Christian (~-0.6, -1.3), Muslim (~-0.7, -1.7)
- USA: Protestant (~1.3, -0.8), Catholic (~1.2, -0.9)

Two broad zones labeled:
- "Historically Catholic" (left/center area)
- "Historically Protestant" (right area)

Key finding: Cross-national differences are much larger than within-nation differences between religious groups.
