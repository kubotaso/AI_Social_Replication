Figure 8: Survival/Self-Expression Values by Year of Birth for Four Types of Societies

Type: Line graph
X-axis: Birth Year (same 7 cohorts as Figure 7)
Y-axis: Survival/Self-Expression Dimension (range approximately -1.0 to 1.0)
High values = self-expression; low values = survival.

Four lines, one for each society type:
1. Advanced industrial democracies (dashed line, starts around 0.3 and rises to ~0.8)
2. Developing societies (dotted line, starts around -0.2 and rises gently to ~0.1)
3. Low-income societies (thin line with markers, relatively flat around -0.3 to 0.0)
4. Ex-Communist societies (solid thick line, starts around -0.3 and DECLINES to about -0.7)

Key findings:
- Advanced industrial societies show strongest shift toward self-expression in younger cohorts
- Ex-Communist societies show OPPOSITE trend: younger cohorts emphasize SURVIVAL more
  (this reflects the collapse of Communism and economic hardship in the 1990s)
- Developing and low-income societies show modest changes

Data: Most recent survey for each country. Data weighted to give each society equal weight.
Same society groupings as Figure 7.
