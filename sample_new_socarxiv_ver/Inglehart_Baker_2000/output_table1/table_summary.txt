Table 1: Items Characterizing Two Dimensions of Cross-Cultural Variation: Nation-Level Analysis

Source: Nation-level and individual-level data from 65 societies surveyed in the 1990-1991 and 1995-1998 World Values Surveys.

Method: Factor analysis with varimax rotation on 10 items. Analysis performed at both nation-level (N=65 cases) and individual-level (N for individual level is 165,594; smallest N for any of the items is 146,789).

Structure: Two dimensions, each with 5 items. Factor loadings reported for both nation-level and individual-level.

DIMENSION 1: Traditional vs. Secular-Rational Values
(Traditional values emphasize the following; secular-rational values emphasize the opposite)

Notes:
- The original polarities vary. The statements show how each item relates to the given dimension.
- Based on a factor analysis with varimax rotation.
- Number of cases for nation-level analysis is 65; total N for individual-level is 165,594 (smallest N for any of the above items is 146,789).
- Dimension 1 explains 44% of cross-national variation and 26% of individual-level variation.
- Dimension 2 explains 26% of cross-national variation and 13% of individual-level variation.
- The Autonomy index is measured by child qualities: obedience vs independence/determination.
- Materialist/postmaterialist measured by the four-item materialist/postmaterialist values index.
