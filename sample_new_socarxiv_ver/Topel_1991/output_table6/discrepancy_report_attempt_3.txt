CORE ISSUE: The IV regression is not properly identifying beta_1.

Our beta_1 = 0.010 vs Topel's 0.0713. This makes beta_2 = 0.123 instead of 0.0545.

The problem is that initial_experience is a near-perfect predictor of current
experience (F-stat > 200,000), so the IV essentially gives OLS results.
OLS produces a very low coefficient on experience because it's picking up
the cross-sectional correlation where high-experience workers have lower
wages relative to what the true experience profile would suggest.

POSSIBLE FIXES:
1. The second-step regression might need person fixed effects or job-level
   controls to properly identify beta_1
2. The adjusted wage formula might be wrong - maybe should NOT subtract
   the delta (experience polynomial) terms, since those are what we're
   trying to estimate
3. Need to understand that w* = log_wage - gamma(T) - delta(X), not
   w* = log_wage - beta_hat*T - gamma(T) - delta(X)
4. Actually, re-reading Topel: w* = log_wage - beta_hat*T - gamma(T) only
   (subtract tenure effects, NOT experience effects)
   Then regress w* on X + X^2 + X^3 + X^4 + controls
   And beta_1 is the coefficient on linear X from the IV regression

APPROACH FOR ATTEMPT 4:
- Subtract only tenure polynomial from log_wage (not experience polynomial)
- Regress on full experience polynomial + controls
- IV experience polynomial with initial experience polynomial
- This should give the proper experience profile beta_1
