TABLE 6: Effects of Measurement Error and Alternative Instrumental Variables
on Estimated Returns to Job Tenure

Source: Topel (1991), "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority,"
Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1, pp. 145-176.

==============================================================================
DATA AND SAMPLE
==============================================================================

Data source: Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID), 1968-1983
Sample: White males
This table examines sensitivity of results to measurement error corrections
and alternative instrument choices.

==============================================================================
TABLE STRUCTURE
==============================================================================

Type: IV / Two-step estimation table with specification variations
Layout: Two panels

Top panel: Main effect of job tenure beta_2 (4 columns)
Bottom panel: Cumulative returns at 5, 10, 15, 20 years (4 columns x 4 rows)

Column descriptions (instruments used):
  (1): Original tenure data, instruments = (X, T-T_bar, Time)
  (2): Corrected tenure data, instruments = (X, T-T_bar, Time)
  (3): Corrected tenure data, instruments = (X^c, T-T_bar, Time)
  (4): Corrected tenure data, instruments = (X^c, T-T_bar)

Where:
  X = total labor market experience
  T = job tenure
  T_bar = mean tenure in the job spell
  Time = calendar time (year dummies)
  X^c = experience corrected for measurement error
  "Corrected" tenure uses the panel structure to correct for reporting errors

==============================================================================
NOTES
==============================================================================

- Basic specification identical to the baseline table (Table 3)
- Other second-step regressors include: education, union membership,
  disability, residence in SMSA, census region indicators
- Column (1) uses original (uncorrected) tenure data and the baseline
  instrument set; this yields lower estimates than the baseline Table 3
  because it uses different instruments
- Column (2) corrects tenure for measurement error using panel data
  (if tenure reported in year t and t+1 differ by more than 1 year,
  the panel-consistent value is used)
- Column (3) additionally corrects experience for measurement error
- Column (4) drops calendar time from the instrument set, relying only
  on corrected experience and within-job tenure deviation
- The estimates increase from column (1) to (4), suggesting that
  measurement error in tenure and experience attenuates the estimated
  returns, and that calendar time may be a weak instrument
- The baseline estimates in Table 3 (beta_2 = .0545) are between
  columns (3) and (4), suggesting moderate measurement error effects

==============================================================================
STATISTICAL METHOD
==============================================================================

Same two-step procedure as Table 3, with variations in:
1. Tenure data: original vs. panel-corrected
2. Experience data: original vs. corrected
3. Instrument set: varying combinations of experience, tenure deviation
   from job-spell mean, and calendar time

The measurement error correction for tenure uses the panel structure:
if a worker reports tenure T_t in year t and T_{t+1} in year t+1,
and |T_{t+1} - T_t - 1| > 0, the tenure sequence is adjusted for
internal consistency.
