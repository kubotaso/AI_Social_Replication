2. PSID PANEL DATASET
======================
File: data/psid_panel.csv (constructed from raw PSID files)
Source files: psid_raw/fam{1968-1983}/ (16 family files) + psid_raw/ind2023er/ (individual file)
Records: 13,993 person-year observations (paper target: 13,128)
Unique persons: 2,407 (paper target: 1,540)
Unique jobs: 3,154 (paper target: 3,228)
Year range: 1971-1983 (post-tenure-restriction; full panel has 1968-1983)

Key variables present:
  - person_id, job_id, year
  - age, education_clean, experience
  - log_hourly_wage, hourly_wage, labor_inc, hours
  - married, union_member, disabled, lives_in_smsa
  - region (NE, NC, South, West dummies)
  - occ_1digit, agriculture, self_employed, govt_worker
  - tenure_topel (reconstructed), new_job
  - d_log_wage (first difference of log wage within job)

Known issues:
  1. Education variable: Raw PSID uses categorical codes (0-8) in early years,
     not actual years of schooling. Mean education = 6.1 (coded) vs paper's 12.645 (years).
     NEEDS RECODING: Map categorical codes to midpoint years.
  2. Unique persons (2,407) exceeds paper's 1,540 by ~56%. Job change detection
     may be too aggressive (marking too many new jobs, splitting persons across jobs).
  3. Government employment filter only available from 1975+; early years may retain
     some government workers.
  4. The tenure reconstruction starts at 0 for first observed year in a job;
     for jobs in progress at panel start, starting tenure is not correctly gauged
     from maximum reported tenure (per Topel's procedure).
  5. No Alaska/Hawaii exclusion implemented (need state variable mapping).
  6. CPS wage index deflation not yet applied to hourly wages.

Status: PARTIALLY READY — usable for initial replication attempts but needs
refinement of education coding, tenure reconstruction, and wage deflation.
