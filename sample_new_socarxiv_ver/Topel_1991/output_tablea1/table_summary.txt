TABLE A1: Variable Definitions and Summary Statistics, PSID White Males, 1968-83

Source: Topel (1991), "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority,"
Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1, pp. 145-176.
Table appears on page 174 of the paper.

==============================================================================
DATA AND SAMPLE
==============================================================================

Data source: Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID), 1968-1983 (first 16 waves)
Sample: White male heads of households (pooled cross-sections across all waves)

Sample restrictions (from Appendix, p. 173):
- White males only
- Ages 18-60 at the survey date
- Positive earnings in the previous calendar year
- Persons from Alaska and Hawaii excluded
- Jobs excluded if respondent reported: self-employment at any time during
  the job, government employment, agricultural employment
- Poverty subsample of PSID excluded (random/SRC sample only)
- Current job tenure >= 1 year (observations with < 1 year tenure deleted)
- Jobs with ambiguous tenure sequences deleted

Final sample size (from p. 148):
- 1,540 individuals
- 3,228 jobs
- 13,128 job-year observations (person-year observations)

==============================================================================
NOTES
==============================================================================

- Real wage is constructed by deflating average hourly earnings first by
  a CPS real wage index (from Murphy and Welch 1987, to account for changes
  in the composition of the CPS sample over time) and then by the GNP price
  deflator for consumption expenditure (to convert to real dollars).
  The result is in LOG form (natural logarithm).
- The CPS Real Wage Index is normalized to 1.000 in 1968 (base year).
  It rises through the mid-1970s (peak 1.188 in 1975) and then declines,
  reflecting real wage stagnation of the late 1970s and early 1980s.
- Experience = Age - Education - 6 (years since leaving school,
  assuming individuals start school at age 6 and enter the labor
  market immediately upon completing their education)
- Tenure is RECONSTRUCTED, not raw reported tenure from the PSID.
  For jobs that began during the panel, tenure is started at zero and
  incremented by one for each year the person works. For jobs in progress
  at the start of the panel, starting tenure is gauged relative to the
  period in which maximum reported tenure was observed. (See Appendix pp. 173-174.)
- Union status: "1 if union member" -- jobs were categorized as "union" if the
  respondent indicated union membership in more than half of the years of
  the job (footnote 18, p. 164)
- The sample percentage column shows that earlier years have slightly fewer
  observations and later years have slightly more, reflecting panel additions
  over time
- All statistics are computed on the pooled sample across all 16 waves
  (13,128 person-year observations)
- Wages refer to average hourly earnings in the year preceding the survey
- Other regressors used in the paper's regression models but not shown
  in this table: 8 census region dummies, year dummies (for 1968-1983)

==============================================================================
VARIABLE CONSTRUCTION DETAILS
==============================================================================

Real wage construction:
  1. Start with average hourly earnings from PSID (wages and salary / annual hours)
  2. Divide by CPS Real Wage Index for the survey year (from bottom panel)
  3. Divide by GNP price deflator for consumption expenditure
  4. Take the natural logarithm
  Result: "Log average hourly earnings deflated by CPS wage index and GNP price deflator"

Experience construction:
  Experience = Age - Years of education - 6
  This assumes individuals start school at age 6 and enter the labor
  market immediately upon completing their education.

Tenure construction (RECONSTRUCTED):
  For jobs starting during the panel: tenure = 0 at start, +1 each year worked
  For jobs in progress at panel start: starting tenure gauged from period of
    maximum reported tenure on the job
  Year-to-year changes in raw reported tenure range from -31 to 7.5 years;
    in 324 cases (3.8%), measured tenure declines between years of a job,
    and in 51 cases the decline exceeds 5 years.
  Jobs with significant ambiguities in tenure sequences were deleted.

Married: 1 if currently married with spouse present, 0 otherwise
Union: 1 if union member in more than half of years on the job, 0 otherwise
SMSA: 1 if resides in Standard Metropolitan Statistical Area, 0 otherwise
Disabled: 1 if currently reporting a disability, 0 otherwise

==============================================================================
STATISTICAL METHOD
==============================================================================

This table is purely descriptive -- it reports sample means and standard
deviations for the key variables, plus the distribution of observations
across survey years and the CPS Real Wage Index values used for deflation.

No statistical model is estimated. The scoring rubric for descriptive tables
applies:
- Categories present (20 pts): All 8 variables in top panel, all 16 years
  in bottom panel
- Count/percentage values (40 pts): Means, SDs, percentages, and wage
  index values match within 2% of true values
- Ordering (10 pts): Variables and years in correct order
- Sample size (20 pts): Implied N from percentages consistent with
  13,128 total observations
- Column structure (10 pts): Same columns as the paper (Variable,
  Definition, Mean, Std Dev for top panel; Year, Pct, Index for bottom)

==============================================================================
SCORING NOTES
==============================================================================

Key values to match precisely:
- All 8 variable means and 8 standard deviations in the top panel
- All 16 percentage-of-sample values in the bottom panel
- All 16 CPS Real Wage Index values in the bottom panel
- Total: 16 + 32 = 48 individual numerical values

The CPS Real Wage Index values are externally given (from Murphy and Welch 1987)
and should be hardcoded. The sample percentages and variable means/SDs must be
computed from the PSID data.
