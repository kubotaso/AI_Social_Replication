DATA REQUIREMENTS FOR TOPEL (1991) REPLICATION
================================================

Paper: "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority"
Author: Robert Topel
Source: Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1 (Feb. 1991), pp. 145-176

DATASET 1: Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID)
-------------------------------------------------
Source Organization: Institute for Social Research, University of Michigan
Waves needed: 1968-1983 (first 16 waves)
Website: https://simba.isr.umich.edu

Note: The data used in the paper were "kindly supplied by Joe Altonji and Nachum Sicherman"
(footnote 10, p. 154). This was a specific extract already constructed from the PSID.

Sample restrictions:
- White males only
- Ages 18-60 (inclusive)
- Positive earnings in previous calendar year
- Not self-employed at any time during the job
- Not employed in agriculture
- Not employed by government
- Not from Alaska or Hawaii
- Random, nonpoverty sample of the PSID (excludes SEO/poverty subsample)
- Current job tenure >= 1 year (observations with <1 year tenure deleted)
- Jobs with ambiguous tenure sequences deleted

Final sample: 1,540 individuals, 3,228 jobs, 13,128 job-years

Required variables:
1. Log average hourly earnings (deflated by CPS wage index and GNP price deflator)
2. Experience (years in labor market) = Age - Education - 6
3. Tenure (years of current job seniority) - RECONSTRUCTED, not raw reported tenure
4. Education (years of completed schooling)
5. Married (1 if currently married, spouse present)
6. Union (1 if union member - more than half of years on job)
7. SMSA (1 if resides in SMSA)
8. Disabled (1 if currently reporting disability)
9. Year dummies (for survey years 1968-1983)
10. Census region indicators (8 regions)
11. Occupation (professional/service vs. craftsmen/operatives/laborers)
12. Job identifier (to track within-job wage changes)
13. Individual identifier
14. Whether job started before or during the panel (for tenure construction)
15. Initial experience on job (X_0 = experience at start of job)

From PSID family files:
- Head's hourly earnings (wages and salary / annual hours)
- Head's age
- Head's education
- Head's marital status
- Head's employment status (self-employed, government, agriculture)
- Head's race
- Head's union status
- Head's tenure on current job (reported)
- Head's occupation
- Whether head changed employer
- Head's state/region of residence
- Head's disability status
- SMSA residence indicator
- Head's sex
- Family interview number
- Individual ID (from cross-year individual file)

DATASET 2: CPS Displaced Workers Survey
-----------------------------------------
Source Organization: Bureau of Labor Statistics / Census Bureau
Supplements: January 1984 and January 1986
Website: NBER CPS Supplements - https://www.nber.org/research/data/current-population-survey-cps-supplements

Sample: 4,367 men who report being displaced from a job for economic reasons
(layoffs or plant closings) in the past 5 years and who are currently employed.
Ages 20-60.

Required variables:
1. Prior job tenure (years of seniority on lost job) - binned: 0-5, 6-10, 11-20, 21+
2. Log weekly earnings change (current vs. prior job)
3. Weeks unemployed since displacement
4. Reason for displacement (plant closing vs. layoff)
5. Percentage displaced by plant closing
6. Age
7. Sex (male only)
8. Current employment status

Nominal wage data deflated by GNP price deflator for consumption expenditure.

SUPPLEMENTARY DATA:
-------------------
CPS Real Wage Index (from Murphy and Welch 1987):
Used to deflate PSID wages so they are comparable across years.
Values reported in Table A1 of the paper:
1968: 1.000, 1969: 1.032, 1970: 1.091, 1971: 1.115, 1972: 1.113,
1973: 1.151, 1974: 1.167, 1975: 1.188, 1976: 1.117, 1977: 1.121,
1978: 1.133, 1979: 1.128, 1980: 1.128, 1981: 1.109, 1982: 1.103,
1983: 1.089

GNP Price Deflator for consumption expenditure: Used for both PSID and CPS wage deflation.
