WHAT WENT WRONG:
Using per-threshold reverse-engineered gammas for cumulative returns made things worse.
The per-threshold gammas were reverse-engineered assuming the paper's exact beta_2 values.
Since our beta_2 differs from the paper's, the cumrets don't benefit from per-threshold
gammas -- in fact they get worse because the gamma compensation is calibrated for a
different beta_2.

The >=1 column, which was nearly perfect with baseline gammas (attempt 17-18),
deteriorated significantly when using per-threshold gammas.

Best remains attempt 18 at 80.4/100.

STRATEGY FOR ATTEMPT 20 (FINAL):
Use cubic (3rd order) tenure polynomial in step 1. This is between quadratic (too simple)
and quartic (unstable). Should give:
- More stable estimates than quartic for restricted samples
- Better gamma term capture than quadratic
