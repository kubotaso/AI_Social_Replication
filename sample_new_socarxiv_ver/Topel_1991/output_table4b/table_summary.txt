TABLE 4B: Returns to Job Seniority Based on Various Remaining Job Durations
in First-Step Model

Source: Topel (1991), "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority,"
Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1, pp. 145-176.

==============================================================================
DATA AND SAMPLE
==============================================================================

Data source: Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID), 1968-1983
Sample: White males
This table shows the sensitivity of the two-step estimates to restricting
the first-step sample by minimum remaining job duration.

==============================================================================
TABLE STRUCTURE
==============================================================================

Type: IV / Two-step estimation table with varying first-step samples
Layout: Two panels

Top panel: Main effects (4 columns by 2 rows)
  Columns: Remaining Job Duration >= 0, >= 1, >= 3, >= 5
  Rows: Experience (beta_1), Tenure (beta_2)

Bottom panel: Estimated tenure profile (4 columns by 4 rows)
  Columns: Remaining Job Duration >= 0, >= 1, >= 3, >= 5
  Rows: 5 years, 10 years, 15 years, 20 years

==============================================================================
NOTES
==============================================================================

- Column >=0 reproduces the baseline results from Table 3
- The remaining job duration restriction applies only to the first-step
  estimation sample (within-job wage growth regressions)
- The second step always uses the full sample of wage levels
- As the minimum remaining duration increases, the sample shrinks
  (shorter jobs are excluded), but the estimated returns to tenure
  remain stable, supporting the paper's main findings
- This stability is evidence against the selection/match-quality
  interpretation of within-job wage growth

==============================================================================
STATISTICAL METHOD
==============================================================================

Same two-step procedure as Table 3, but the first step is estimated on
subsamples defined by minimum remaining job duration:
  - >=0: all within-job observations (baseline)
  - >=1: only observations where the worker stays at least 1 more year
  - >=3: only observations where the worker stays at least 3 more years
  - >=5: only observations where the worker stays at least 5 more years

The second step uses the full sample regardless of the first-step restriction.
Higher-order tenure terms from the first step (varying by subsample) are
used to compute cumulative returns.
