ROOT CAUSES OF REMAINING DISCREPANCIES:

1. CUMULATIVE RETURNS (biggest loss: -12.8 points)
   The cumret = beta_2*T + g2*T^2 + g3*T^3 + g4*T^4 computation uses paper's gamma terms
   from the quartic polynomial. But beta_2 = b1+b2 - beta_1, and our beta_1 is too high
   for some thresholds (0.079 vs 0.061-0.072), making beta_2 too low.

   For >=1: beta_2 = 0.1310 - 0.0789 = 0.0521 (close to paper's 0.0546) -> cumrets match
   For >=0: beta_2 = 0.1243 - 0.0792 = 0.0452 (vs paper's 0.0545) -> 0.009 too low -> cumrets low
   For >=5: beta_2 = 0.1141 - 0.0795 = 0.0346 (vs paper's 0.0584) -> 0.024 too low -> cumrets very low

   The fundamental issue: beta_1 barely varies across thresholds (all ~0.079) while the
   paper shows it should drop from 0.079 to 0.061. When b1+b2 decreases (as for >=5),
   beta_2 absorbs the entire decrease instead of being shared with beta_1.

2. BETA_1 NEAR-CONSTANT (secondary issue)
   Our IV regression gives beta_1 ≈ 0.079 regardless of step 1 b1+b2.
   Paper shows: 0.0713, 0.0792, 0.0716, 0.0607
   The gradient d(beta_1)/d(b1b2) ≈ -0.033 is too small to produce meaningful variation.
   This is a structural property of our data/estimation -- may require a different instrument
   or different step 2 sample to change.

3. N MISMATCH (11,492 vs 10,685, 7.6% high)
   HoH+sons sample is 7.6% too large. Pure HoH gives 9,500 (11% low).
   No obvious fix without understanding exactly which person numbers the paper included.
