PSID Variable Mapping for Topel (1991) Replication
====================================================
Generated from .do files in psid_raw/

This document maps variable names from each PSID family file (1968-1983)
to the constructs needed for Topel's analysis.

Notes:
- Variable names follow the PSID convention (V### for family files, ER#### for individual file)
- Column positions are 1-based and inclusive (start-end)
- "Year X income/wages/hours" reported in year Y refers to income EARNED in year X
  e.g., 1969 file reports 1968 income, 1970 file reports 1969 income, etc.
- Education coding changed across waves; see notes per year
- Race coding: 1=White, 2=Black (earlier years); later years may vary

================================================================================
VARIABLE CODING NOTES
================================================================================

Education Coding:
  1968 (V313): Bracket coded (0=0-5 grades, 1=6-8, 2=9-11, 3=12, 4=some coll, 5=BA, 6=adv deg, 9=NA)
  1969-1974: Similar bracket coding
  1975-1983: Bracket coded (0=0-5, 1=6-8, 2=9-11, 3=12, 4=12+non-acad, 5=some coll, 6=BA, 7=adv deg, 9=NA)
  To convert to approximate years:
    0 -> 3 years (midpoint of 0-5)
    1 -> 7 years (midpoint of 6-8)
    2 -> 10 years (midpoint of 9-11)
    3 -> 12 years
    4 -> 13 years (some college)  -- or 12 for non-academic
    5 -> 14 years (some college) or 16 (BA)
    6 -> 16 years (BA) or 17 (advanced)
    7 -> 17 years (advanced degree)

Employment Status:
  1=Working now, 2=Temp laid off, 3=Looking for work, 4=Retired, 5=Disabled,
  6=Keeping house, 7=Student, 8=Other
  Self-employed determined from separate question when available

Race:
  Pre-1972: 1=White, 2=Negro, 3=PR/Mex/Cuban, 7=Other
  1972+: Race carried forward from 1972 interview
  1=White, 2=Black, 3=American Indian, 4=Asian, 5=Latino, 7=Other

Self-Employment:
  In some years a direct variable exists (1=Self-employed, 5=Not)
  In other years, must use Employment Status or check for separate employer type variable

Union Membership:
  Coding varies by year; typically 1=Yes, 5=No or 1=Yes, 0=No
  Some years ask about union coverage vs. membership separately

Same Employer / Job Change:
  Available in: 1976 (partial), 1978, 1981, 1982, 1983
  Coding typically: 1=Yes (same employer), 5=No (different employer), 0=Inap
  For years without this variable, must infer from:
    - Month started current job (if available)
    - Occupation/industry changes
    - These inferences are imperfect

State Codes:
  PSID uses Census/FIPS state codes (2-digit)

SMSA/Metro:
  Variable indicates size of largest city in SMSA/MSA
  0 = not in SMSA, 1-9 = various size categories

SRC/SEO (Sample identification):
  SRC = Survey Research Center sample (national probability/random)
  SEO = Survey of Economic Opportunity oversample (poverty oversample)
  Latino/Immigrant supplement added in 1990+
  For Topel: use SRC sample only (exclude SEO/poverty oversample)
  Coding varies: often embedded in interview number ranges
    - SRC: interview numbers 1-2930 (1968) or similar ranges
    - SEO: interview numbers 5001+ (1968) or similar
  Later years have explicit "SPLIT SAMPLE" variable:
    1=SRC cross-section, 2=SEO/Census, etc.
