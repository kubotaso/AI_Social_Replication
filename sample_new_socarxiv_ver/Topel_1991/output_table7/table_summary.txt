TABLE 7: Least-Squares Models Conditioning on (Estimated) Completed Job Tenure,
PSID White Males

Source: Topel (1991), "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority,"
Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1, pp. 145-176.
Table appears on page 171 of the paper.

==============================================================================
DATA AND SAMPLE
==============================================================================

Data source: Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID), 1968-1983
Sample: White males
Dependent variable: Log average hourly earnings (levels, not first-differenced)

==============================================================================
TABLE STRUCTURE
==============================================================================

Type: OLS regression table (cross-sectional wage levels)
Layout: 5 model columns

These models test the prediction that, if wages rise with tenure due to
specific capital accumulation, then completed job tenure (total length of
the job spell) should have a positive effect on wages even after controlling
for current tenure, since it signals a longer period of investment returns.

Columns (2) and (4): Restricted model (equation 17 in the paper)
Columns (3) and (5): Unrestricted model (equation 18 in the paper)

The difference between columns (2)-(3) and (4)-(5) is whether "observed"
or "imputed" completed tenure is used.

NOTES
==============================================================================

- See note to Table 4 for other regressors (education, marital status,
  SMSA, union, disability, census region dummies)
- Columns (2) and (4) implement the restricted model (equation 17):
  the effect of completed tenure enters only as a level shift
- Columns (3) and (5) implement the unrestricted model (equation 18):
  completed tenure is allowed to interact with current tenure and
  experience, permitting the wage profile to vary with expected job length
- "Observed completed tenure" is the actual observed duration of the job
  spell in the PSID panel (for jobs that end during the panel) or a
  censored measure (for jobs still ongoing at panel's end)
- "Imputed completed tenure" is predicted completed tenure based on a
  hazard model of job ending
- "x censor" is an interaction of completed tenure with a dummy for
  right-censored job spells (job ongoing at end of panel)
- The positive coefficient on completed tenure supports the specific
  capital model: workers in longer-lasting jobs earn more at every
  tenure level, consistent with more investment in specific capital
- Column (1) is a baseline OLS model with no completed tenure controls
- The paper does NOT report N (sample size) for Table 7. The main PSID
  sample has 13,128 person-year observations (p. 148). All 5 columns
  use the same sample.
- NOTE ON Experience^2 interaction SEs: Both columns (3) and (5) have
  6-decimal-place SEs (.000036 and .000033 respectively), matching the
  scale of the main Experience^2 SEs (e.g., .000069, .000030). The PDF
  typography makes it easy to misread the number of leading zeros.
- The paper's note says: "See note to table 4 for other regressors.
  Dependent variable is log average hourly earnings. Standard errors
  are in parentheses. Cols. 2 and 4 implement versions of the restricted
  model given by eq. (17). Estimates in cols. 3 and 5 are based on the
  unrestricted model (18)."

==============================================================================
STATISTICAL METHOD
==============================================================================

OLS regressions of log wage levels on experience, tenure, completed tenure,
and controls. This is a cross-sectional approach (not the two-step estimator)
that tests implications of the specific capital model by examining whether
completed job tenure affects current wages.

Column (1): Basic OLS
  w = X*beta_1 + X^2*beta_x2 + T*beta_2 + controls + epsilon
  (No completed tenure variable)

Columns (2) and (4) - Restricted (equation 17):
  w = X*beta_1 + X^2*beta_x2 + T*beta_2 + T_bar*delta + controls + epsilon
  where T_bar is completed tenure (observed in col 2, imputed in col 4)
  "x censor" (col 2 only): interaction of observed completed tenure with a
  dummy for right-censored job spells

Columns (3) and (5) - Unrestricted (equation 18):
  w = X*beta_1 + X^2*beta_x2 + T*beta_2 + T_bar*delta
      + T_bar*X^2*gamma_x + T_bar*T*gamma_t + controls + epsilon
  Allows completed tenure to interact with Experience^2 and Tenure

Other regressors (see Table 4 note): education, marital status (married),
union membership, SMSA, disability, census region dummies (8 regions).

For the purposes of this table, the models use only a quadratic in
experience and a LINEAR term in tenure (not the higher-order polynomial
used in Tables 3-6). This is the functional form used by Abraham and
Farber (1987) for comparison purposes (p. 171).

Imputed completed tenure (cols 4-5) is predicted from a discrete-time
proportional hazards model of job endings (footnote 24, p. 171). The
hazard rate is lambda = exp(Z*gamma), where Z includes the full vector
of regressors used in the wage models.
