TABLE 1: Wage Changes of Displaced Workers by Years of Prior Job Seniority, January CPS 1984 and 1986

Source: Topel (1991), "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority,"
Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1, pp. 145-176.

==============================================================================
DATA AND SAMPLE
==============================================================================

Data source: CPS Displaced Workers Survey, January 1984 and January 1986
Sample size: 4,367 men
Sample restrictions:
  - Males ages 20-60
  - Displaced from a job for economic reasons (layoffs or plant closings)
    in the past 5 years
  - Currently employed at the time of the survey
Deflation: Nominal data deflated by GNP price deflator for consumption expenditure

==============================================================================
TABLE STRUCTURE
==============================================================================

Type: Descriptive/summary statistics table
Layout: 3 rows x 5 columns (plus standard errors in parentheses)

Columns represent years of seniority on prior job:
  - Column 1: 0-5 years
  - Column 2: 6-10 years
  - Column 3: 11-20 years
  - Column 4: 21+ years
  - Column 5: Total

Rows:
  - Row 1: Average change in log weekly wage (mean and SE)
  - Row 2: Percentage displaced by plant closing (mean and SE)
  - Row 3: Weeks unemployed since displacement (mean and SE)

==============================================================================
NOTES
==============================================================================

- Standard errors are reported in parentheses
- The table shows that wage losses from displacement increase monotonically
  with prior job seniority
- Workers with 21+ years of seniority lose approximately 44% of their wages
  upon displacement
- Plant closings account for a higher fraction of displacements among
  high-seniority workers
- Unemployment duration also increases with prior seniority

==============================================================================
STATISTICAL METHOD
==============================================================================

Simple means and standard errors computed by seniority category.
Standard errors are standard errors of the mean: SE = s / sqrt(n)
where s is the sample standard deviation and n is the number of observations
in each cell.
