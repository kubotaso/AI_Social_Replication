TABLE 2: Models of Annual Within-Job Wage Growth, PSID White Males, 1968-83

Source: Topel (1991), "Specific Capital, Mobility, and Wages: Wages Rise with Job Seniority,"
Journal of Political Economy, Vol. 99, No. 1, pp. 145-176.

==============================================================================
DATA AND SAMPLE
==============================================================================

Data source: Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID), 1968-1983
Sample: White males
Dependent variable: Change in log real wage (first difference); Mean = .026
Number of observations: N = 8,683

Sample restrictions:
  - White males
  - Within-job observations (consecutive years on the same job)
  - First-differenced data (change in log real wage from year t-1 to t)

==============================================================================
TABLE STRUCTURE
==============================================================================

Type: Regression table (OLS on first-differenced data)
Layout: 3 model columns

The dependent variable is the annual change in log real wage.
Independent variables are changes in tenure and experience polynomials.

This is the FIRST STEP of Topel's two-step estimator. By first-differencing,
person fixed effects and job-match effects are eliminated. The coefficients
estimate (beta_1 + beta_2) for tenure terms (the combined effect of experience
and tenure within a job) and pure experience effects for the higher-order
experience terms.

==============================================================================
NOTES
==============================================================================

- All models include year dummies (15 time period indicators for 1969-1983)
- The coefficients on Delta Tenure represent the combined return to tenure
  and experience (beta_1 + beta_2) since within a job, changes in tenure
  and experience are identical (both increase by 1 each year)
- Higher-order experience terms are separately identified because experience
  varies across individuals at any given tenure level
- The predicted wage growth panel shows that annual wage growth declines
  with tenure, from about 6.8% in year 1 to 2.6% in year 10
- Scaling: Experience^2 coefficients are multiplied by 10^2, Experience^3
  by 10^3, Experience^4 by 10^4 (similarly for tenure polynomial terms)

==============================================================================
STATISTICAL METHOD
==============================================================================

OLS regression on first-differenced data.
The first-differencing eliminates individual fixed effects (alpha_i) and
job-match effects (mu_ij) from the wage equation.
The model estimated is:
  Delta w_ijt = (beta_1 + beta_2) * Delta T + g'(X) * Delta X + Delta epsilon_ijt
where Delta T = 1 for within-job observations, X is experience, and g'(X)
contains the higher-order experience terms.
