ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:

The fundamental issue is that our PSID data covers 1971-1983 (13 waves)
while the paper uses 1968-1983 (16 waves). The paper's data was
"kindly supplied by Joe Altonji and Nachum Sicherman" (footnote 10, p.154),
suggesting pre-processed data with potentially different sample selections.

Specific impacts:
1. Missing 1968-1970 data means:
   - Fewer total observations (we have 2,407 persons vs paper's 1,540)
   - Different experience distribution (more persons enter mid-career)
   - Missing low-tenure/high-growth early career observations

2. Experience polynomial coefficients are FUNDAMENTALLY affected by the
   experience distribution. With VIF > 500 for d_exp_cu, the individual
   polynomial coefficients are not well-identified in our data. Small
   changes in sample composition cause large changes in coefficients.

3. The paper likely has:
   - More observations at low experience levels (young workers entering 1968)
   - Stricter sample restrictions (1,540 vs our 1,951 persons)
   - Better initial tenure calibration from more years of reported data

POSSIBLE IMPROVEMENTS FOR NEXT ATTEMPT:
1. Try to restrict to fewer persons to match the paper's 1,540 persons
2. Try tighter experience bounds (e.g., experience 2-35)
3. Try using the full panel (psid_panel_full.csv) which has 1970
4. Try a different outlier approach (percentile-based or SD-based)
5. Consider if there are additional sample restrictions from the paper
   we haven't implemented (e.g., Alaska/Hawaii exclusion, age 18-60)
