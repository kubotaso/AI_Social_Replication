WHAT THE TENURE ISSUE REALLY MEANS:
- The paper's Delta Tenure coefficient of ~0.127 implies ~12.7% annual return
  to tenure at the start of a job
- Our value of ~0.200 implies ~20% annual return
- This is likely because our tenure distribution is compressed (starts at 1)
  while the paper's may include pre-panel tenure giving higher starting values
- With higher tenure values, the quadratic/cubic/quartic terms absorb more
  of the effect, bringing the linear coefficient down

STRATEGIES FOR NEXT ATTEMPT:
1. Try a DIFFERENT tenure construction: instead of starting at 1 for all
   first observations, try to infer initial tenure from job start dates
   or from the age/experience at first observation
2. Try adding tenure^2/3/4 LEVELS to the model (not just differences)
   to see if this changes the first-differenced coefficients
3. Try scaling tenure differently - the paper reports tenure in years
   while we compute in years from months
4. Try using a Tobit-style correction or sample selection approach
5. Consider that the paper's 8,683 observations may use different
   year coverage or different sample restrictions we haven't identified
