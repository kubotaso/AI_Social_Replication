COMMON PSID VARIABLE CONSTRUCTION (applies to Tables 2-7, A1)
================================================================

1. Log hourly wage (dependent variable base):
   - hourly_wage = labor_inc / hours (already computed in panel)
   - log_hourly_wage = ln(hourly_wage) (already computed)
   - DEFLATION: log_real_wage = log_hourly_wage - ln(CPS_wage_index[year])
   - CPS Wage Index values: {1968:1.000, 1969:1.032, 1970:1.091, 1971:1.115,
     1972:1.113, 1973:1.151, 1974:1.167, 1975:1.188, 1976:1.117, 1977:1.121,
     1978:1.133, 1979:1.128, 1980:1.128, 1981:1.109, 1982:1.103, 1983:1.089}

2. Education (years of completed schooling):
   - Panel column: education_clean
   - CRITICAL: In early PSID waves (1968-1975), education is CATEGORICAL:
     0=Can't read/write, 1=0-5 grades, 2=6-8 grades, 3=9-11 grades,
     4=12 grades (HS), 5=12+nonacademic, 6=some college, 7=BA degree, 8=advanced degree
   - MUST recode to approximate years:
     0->0, 1->3, 2->7, 3->10, 4->12, 5->12, 6->14, 7->16, 8->17
   - In later waves (1976+), education may already be in years (0-17)
   - Check: if max(education) <= 8, it's categorical; if max > 8, it's years

3. Experience:
   - experience = age - education_years - 6
   - Must use recoded education (years), not raw categorical code
   - Ensure experience >= 0

4. Tenure (reconstructed):
   - Panel column: tenure_topel
   - Starts at 0 for first year observed in a job, increments by 1
   - For jobs in progress at panel start: use max reported tenure to gauge starting value

5. Year dummies: year_1968 through year_1983 (already in panel)

6. Region dummies: region_ne, region_nc, region_south, region_west (already in panel)

================================================================
TABLE 2 SPECIFIC INSTRUCTIONS
================================================================

Dependent variable: Change in log real wage (first difference within job)
  d_log_real_wage = log_real_wage[t] - log_real_wage[t-1]
  Only for consecutive within-job observations (same job_id, consecutive years)
  Mean should be approximately .026

Sample: Within-job observations only (where d_log_wage is non-missing)
  N should be approximately 8,683

Independent variables (all in FIRST-DIFFERENCED form):
  - Delta Tenure (linear): always = 1 within a job (tenure increases by 1 each year)
  - Delta Tenure^2 (x10^2): (tenure_t^2 - tenure_{t-1}^2) * 100
  - Delta Tenure^3 (x10^3): difference of cubed tenure * 1000
  - Delta Tenure^4 (x10^4): difference of quartic tenure * 10000
  - Delta Experience^2 (x10^2): (exp_t^2 - exp_{t-1}^2) * 100
  - Delta Experience^3 (x10^3): (exp_t^3 - exp_{t-1}^3) * 1000
  - Delta Experience^4 (x10^4): (exp_t^4 - exp_{t-1}^4) * 10000
  - Year dummies (15 dummies for 1969-1983; first-differenced = changes in year dummies)

Note on scaling: The paper reports coefficients with polynomial terms scaled by
powers of 10. E.g., "Delta Experience^2 (x10^2)" means the coefficient is for
experience^2 / 100. So if raw coefficient is -0.006051, the reported value is
-.6051 (multiplied by 100).

Three models:
  Model (1): Linear tenure + quartic experience + year dummies
  Model (2): Quadratic tenure + quartic experience + year dummies
  Model (3): Quartic tenure + quartic experience + year dummies

Bottom panel: Predicted wage growth at tenure 1-10 for worker with 10 years experience
  Using Model (3) coefficients.

Statistical method: OLS on first-differenced data.
