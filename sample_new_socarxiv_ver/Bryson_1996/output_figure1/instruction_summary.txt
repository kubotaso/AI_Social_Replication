CONSTRUCTION STEPS:

1. PREPARE DATA:
   - Load CSV, keep only respondents with all 18 genres rated 1-5 (listwise deletion on genres)
   - Education variable: educ

2. FOR EACH GENRE (18 iterations):
   a. Create binary DV: 1 if genre rating >= 4 (dislike or dislike very much), 0 if rating <= 3
   b. Create musical tolerance IV: count of the OTHER 17 genres where rating < 4 (i.e., not disliked)
      Equivalently: 17 minus (count of other 17 genres rated >= 4)
      The key point: EXCLUDE the current genre from the tolerance calculation
   c. Run LOGISTIC regression: log(odds of disliking genre) = b0 + b1*tolerance + b2*education
   d. Record b1 (the tolerance coefficient) for this genre

3. COMPUTE MEAN EDUCATION OF FANS:
   For each genre, compute mean(educ) among respondents who rated the genre 1 or 2 ("like very much" or "like it")
   Use the full sample (or at least those with valid education data), not restricted to the 912 with all genres valid

4. COMPUTE SAMPLE MEAN EDUCATION:
   Mean of educ for the analysis sample

5. RANK GENRES:
   Sort genres by tolerance coefficient (ascending: most negative first = most affected by tolerance)
   The order from the paper (left to right) goes from genres LEAST excluded by tolerant people to MOST excluded

6. PLOT:
   - X-axis: genres in ranked order
   - Left Y-axis: tolerance coefficients (solid line with markers)
     Range approximately -0.5 to -0.1
   - Right Y-axis: mean education of fans (dashed line)
     Range approximately 12 to 15
   - Horizontal reference line: sample mean education (~13 years)
   - X-axis labels rotated for readability

   Paper's axis labels:
   - Left Y: "Coefficients for Musical Tolerance as It Affects One's Chances of Disliking Each Music Genre"
   - Right Y: "Mean Educational Level of Respondents Who Reported Liking Each Music Genre"
   - X: "Type of Music"
   - Legend: "Mean Education of Genre Audience" (dashed), "Sample Mean Education" (dotted horizontal), "Coefficient of Musical Tolerance" (solid)

VISUAL STYLE:
- The figure shows the solid line generally going from less negative (left) to more negative (right)
- The dashed line shows higher education on the left and lower education on the right
- This demonstrates that genres with higher-educated audiences are least rejected by tolerant respondents
