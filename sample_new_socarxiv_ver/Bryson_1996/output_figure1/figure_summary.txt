Figure 1: The Effect of Being Musically Tolerant on Disliking Each Music Genre Compared to the Educational Composition of Genre Audiences

Structure: Dual-axis line plot
- X-axis: 18 music genres, ordered by the musical tolerance coefficient (from most negative to least negative)
- Left Y-axis: Coefficients for Musical Tolerance (solid line with dots)
- Right Y-axis: Mean Educational Level of Respondents Who Reported Liking Each Music Genre (dashed line)

The figure also shows a horizontal reference line at the sample mean education level.

=== CONSTRUCTION METHOD ===

Step 1: For each of the 18 music genres, run a LOGISTIC REGRESSION:
- DV: Whether respondent dislikes that genre (1 = dislike or dislike very much [rating 4 or 5], 0 = otherwise [rating 1, 2, or 3])
  EXCLUDE the genre being predicted from the tolerance score calculation
- IV1: Musical tolerance score = number of genres NOT disliked (out of remaining 17 genres)
  This is the REVERSE of the exclusiveness score minus the current genre
  Specifically: tolerance = (17 - count of OTHER genres rated 4 or 5)
  Or equivalently: tolerance = count of OTHER genres rated 1, 2, or 3
- IV2: Education (years)
- The coefficient of interest is the one for musical tolerance (the log odds)

Step 2: Rank genres by their musical tolerance coefficient (ascending, most negative first)

Step 3: For each genre, compute the mean education of respondents who reported LIKING that genre
- "Liking" = rating of 1 (like very much) or 2 (like it)
- Paper says "mean educational level of respondents who reported liking each music genre"

Step 4: Plot both series:
- X-axis: genres in order from Step 2
- Left Y-axis: musical tolerance coefficients (solid line), approximately range -0.5 to -0.1
- Right Y-axis: mean education of fans (dashed line), approximately range 12 to 15
- Horizontal dotted line at sample mean education
