Table 4 — "Variance Decompositions of Forecasted Variables"

Source: Bernanke and Blinder (1992), Table 4, p. 910

Description:
This is the variance decomposition analogue of Table 3, using CPBILL and TERM instead of BILL and BOND.
Each row is a separate 7-variable VAR: forecasted_var + CPI + M1 + M2 + CPBILL + TERM + FUNDS.
Variance decompositions are computed at a 24-month forecast horizon.
Each VAR is estimated with 6 lags.

The difference between Panels A and B is the Choleski ordering of FUNDS and CPBILL:
- Panel A: CPBILL is placed ahead of FUNDS among the policy variables.
- Panel B: FUNDS is placed ahead of CPBILL among the policy variables.

Per footnote 23 (p. 910): "The ordering underlying the two panels of Table 4 puts the
monetary-policy variables (as a group) first, followed by own lags and the CPI."

Panel A Choleski ordering: M1, M2, CPBILL, TERM, FUNDS, forecasted_var, CPI
Panel B Choleski ordering: M1, M2, FUNDS, TERM, CPBILL, forecasted_var, CPI

Notes:
- 24-month forecast horizon for all decompositions
- Each row is a separate 7-variable VAR estimated with 6 lags
- Panel A Choleski ordering: M1, M2, CPBILL, TERM, FUNDS, forecasted_var, CPI
  (CPBILL placed ahead of FUNDS)
- Panel B Choleski ordering: M1, M2, FUNDS, TERM, CPBILL, forecasted_var, CPI
  (FUNDS placed ahead of CPBILL)
- "See notes to Table 2" — same general methodology as Table 2
- The key finding: when FUNDS is ordered first (Panel B), it absorbs predictive power
  from CPBILL and TERM, suggesting the funds rate captures independent policy information
- OWN and CPI contributions are identical across panels (they are ordered last)
- M1 and M2 contributions are also identical across panels (they are ordered first)
- Only CPBILL, TERM, and FUNDS contributions change between panels due to reordering

Forecasted variables (same as Tables 1-3):
1. Industrial production (log IP, first difference)
2. Capacity utilization (level)
3. Employment (log payroll employment, first difference)
4. Unemployment rate (level)
5. Housing starts (log housing starts, first difference)
6. Personal income (log real personal income, first difference)
7. Retail sales (not specified if real or nominal)
8. Consumption (not specified if real or nominal)
9. Durable-goods orders (log durable goods orders, first difference)
