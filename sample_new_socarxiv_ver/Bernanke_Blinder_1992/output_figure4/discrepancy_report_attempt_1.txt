Discrepancy Report -- Figure 4, Attempt 1
==========================================

Score: 30/100 (manual assessment; automated score was 67 but inflated due to shape checks passing while values are completely wrong)

MAJOR ISSUES:
1. ALL IRF values are far too large in magnitude:
   - Unemployment: reaches +54 at month 24 (should be +0.17)
   - Deposits: reaches -2.2 at month 12 (should be -0.80)
   - Securities: reaches -2.0 at month 8 (should be -0.83)
   - Loans: reaches -3.4 at month 24 (should be -1.35)

2. The scaling factor of 100 is applied on top of already over-large IRF values.
   The bank variables are about 2.5-3x too large, and unemployment is about 300x too large.

3. The funds_rate innovation std dev is 0.3375, close to the paper's 0.31. So the
   model is roughly correct but something about the IRF extraction is wrong.

ANALYSIS:
- The raw IRF from statsmodels for a 1-std-dev orthogonalized shock to funds_rate
  gives the response in the same units as each variable.
- For unemployment (in %-points), the raw response at month 24 is ~0.54 %-pts.
  Multiply by 100 gives 54. But the paper shows 0.17 on the x10^-2 scale.
  So the paper's value of 0.17 means 0.17 * 10^-2 = 0.0017 %-pts.
  My raw value is 0.54, which is 300x too large.

- For bank variables (in log levels), the raw response at month 12 for deposits
  is about -0.021. Multiply by 100 gives -2.1. Paper shows -0.8 on the x10^-2 scale.
  So the paper expects -0.008 in raw log-point terms. My raw is -0.021, about 2.6x too large.

HYPOTHESIS:
The issue might be related to how the orthogonalized IRFs are computed. The paper may
be using unit shocks (1 basis point or 1% shock) rather than 1-std-dev shocks, or
the Choleski decomposition might be different from what statsmodels implements.

Alternatively, there might be an issue with the variable definitions - perhaps the
bank variables need to be in different units, or the VAR needs to be estimated differently.
