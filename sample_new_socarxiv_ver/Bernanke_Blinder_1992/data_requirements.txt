Data Requirements for Bernanke and Blinder (1992) Replication
================================================================

Paper: "The Federal Funds Rate and the Channels of Monetary Transmission"
Authors: Ben S. Bernanke and Alan S. Blinder
Source: American Economic Review, 82(4), 901-921, September 1992

Data Frequency: Monthly (primary), Weekly (Section III only)
All data seasonally adjusted except interest rates.
Original source: DRI (Data Resources, Inc.) database.

DERIVED VARIABLES
=================

- FFBOND = FUNDS - GS10 (funds rate minus 10-year bond rate)
- CPBILL = CP6M - TB6MS (6-month commercial paper rate minus 6-month T-bill rate)
  Note: TB6MS = 6-month T-bill rate; if unavailable, approximate from TB3MS
- TERM = GS10 - GS1 (10-year minus 1-year Treasury rate)
- Real variables: deflated by CPI (base 1982-84=100)

MODEL SPECIFICATIONS
====================

Tables 1-2: 6-variable prediction equations
  - Forecasted variable + CPI (log) + M1 (log) + M2 (log) + interest rate + 6 lags each
  - Constant included
  - Real variables and CPI in log levels

Tables 3-4: 6-variable prediction equations
  - Forecasted variable + CPI (log) + M1 (log) + M2 (log) + CPBILL + TERM + FUNDS + 6 lags each
  - Wait, Table 3 columns are: M1, M2, CPBILL, TERM, FUNDS
  - These are 6-variable equations (own lags + CPI + the 5 monetary/interest variables)

Table 5: Modified Avery MIMIC model
  - Latent variable y* = Xc + u, where X = 6 lags of unemployment + 6 lags of inflation
  - Indicators Z: FFBOND, DRBOND, annualized real growth rate of nonborrowed reserves
  - Normalization: coefficient of FFBOND in Z equation = 1
  - Sample: 1959:8 - 1979:9
  - Variables measured as deviations from means, no constant

Table 6: IV regression of slope of reserve supply function
  - Regress innovation in policy measure on innovation in nonborrowed reserves
  - Instruments: innovations from 3 macro variables in 5-var VARs
  - Sample: 1959:8 - 1979:9

Figure 2: 3-variable VAR impulse responses
  - Variables: FUNDS, unemployment rate (male 25-54), log CPI
  - 6 lags, pre-October 1979 sample
  - Ordering: FUNDS first (policy variable, then unemployment, then inflation)
  - Actually footnote 28 says ordering is: policy variable, unemployment rate, inflation rate
  - One-standard-deviation shocks
  - Responses of FUNDS to unemployment and inflation shocks

Figure 4: 5-variable VAR impulse responses
  - Variables: FUNDS, unemployment rate, log CPI, log(bank balance sheet variable / CPI)
  - 6 lags, sample 1959:1 - 1978:12
  - Three separate VARs, one for each bank variable (deposits, securities, loans)
  - Responses to a one-standard-deviation FUNDS shock
  - Ordering: FUNDS first, then unemployment, CPI, bank variable
  - Actually need to check ordering more carefully
