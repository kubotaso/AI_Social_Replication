KNOWN ISSUES
============

1. Durable goods orders: No FRED series covers 1959-1989 for manufacturing
   new orders of durable goods. Historical NBER series M0684CUSM144NNBR ends
   June 1969. Modern DGORDER starts February 1992. This affects one row
   (durable-goods orders) in Tables 1 and 2.

2. CPBILL approximation: Using H0RIFSPPFM06NM (6-month finance paper placed
   directly) instead of DRI's RMCML6NS (prime commercial paper, 6 months).
   These are closely related but not identical. The finance paper rate was a
   discount rate, while the DRI series was a dealer offering rate. Small
   differences in levels but spreads should be similar.

3. Bank balance-sheet data: Using FRED series LOANS (bank loans and leases)
   and INVEST (investment securities) rather than the paper's original Fed
   Banking and Monetary Statistics data. The FRED H.8 series begins 1973;
   the LOANS and INVEST series go back to 1947 but definitions may differ
   slightly from the paper's "Principal Assets and Liabilities" table.

4. Data vintage: All series are current vintage (as of 2026), while the paper
   used data available circa 1990-1991. Revisions to industrial production,
   employment, income, and monetary aggregates may cause point estimates to
   differ.

5. Retail sales: Using FRED RSALES instead of DRI STR82 (real retail sales
   in 1982 dollars). We deflate RSALES by CPI to get real values.

ASSESSMENT: Data is sufficient for replication of all 9 targets, with durable
goods orders as the one gap (affects 1 row in 2 tables). Proceed to Step 2.
