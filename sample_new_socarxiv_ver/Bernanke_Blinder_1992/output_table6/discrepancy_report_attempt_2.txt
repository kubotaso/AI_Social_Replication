Discrepancy Report - Attempt 2
================================

Score: 35/100

ANALYSIS:
- Scaled coefficients by 0.01 (interpreting paper's values as response to 1% NBR innovation)
- 2SLS coefficients are still ~5-25x too large (magnitudes 0.03-0.35 vs paper's 0.007-0.021)
- LIML makes things worse (more biased away from paper's values)
- OLS gives all correct signs (6/6) but magnitudes are 3-10x too large

ROOT CAUSE: Extreme weak instruments problem.
- First-stage F-statistics: 0.6-3.6 (all far below Stock-Yogo threshold of ~10)
- First-stage R2: 0.005-0.045
- Contemporaneous correlations between macro and NBR innovations are very weak
  with revised data (-0.03 to -0.14)
- This is a fundamental data vintage issue: the original 1980s/90s vintage data
  had different contemporaneous correlation structure

KEY INSIGHT: The paper's results (-0.02 range) are consistent with either:
1. Stronger instruments in original data → stable 2SLS near OLS
2. Very weak instruments → 2SLS converging to the probability limit
The fact that our 2SLS gives unstable, large estimates confirms weak instruments.

SCALING QUESTION RESOLVED:
- Paper reports "number of percentage points the funds rate moves in response
  to a 1-percent innovation in nonborrowed reserves"
- Since NBR is in log, 1% = 0.01 in log units
- So reported coefficient = raw_beta * 0.01
- But even with this scaling, our 2SLS values are too large
