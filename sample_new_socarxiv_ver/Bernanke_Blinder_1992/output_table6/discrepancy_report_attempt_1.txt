Discrepancy Report - Attempt 1
================================

Score: 35/100

MAJOR ISSUE: All coefficients are orders of magnitude too large.
- Paper reports values around -0.02 (in "percentage points per 1-percent NBR innovation")
- Generated values are around -13 to -35 (raw regression coefficients)

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
1. The paper reports scaled coefficients: "Each entry is the number of percentage points
   the funds rate or funds-rate spread moves in response to a 1-percent innovation in
   nonborrowed reserves." Since NBR is in logs, a 1% innovation = 0.01 in log units.
   The reported value = raw_beta * 0.01.

2. However, even after scaling by 0.01:
   - Set A FUNDS: -0.131 (vs paper's -0.021)
   - This is still ~6x too large

3. The fundamental problem is WEAK INSTRUMENTS. The first-stage F-statistic is only 2.06
   (R2 = 0.026). With current data vintages, the macro innovations have very low
   contemporaneous correlation with NBR innovations (-0.12, -0.14, -0.03).

4. Exploration showed that at 12 lags, the scaled beta becomes -0.020, very close to
   the paper's -0.021. This suggests the paper's original data vintage had different
   contemporaneous correlations, making the instruments stronger.

5. The OLS coefficient (scaled) is -0.056, which is closer to the paper's values than
   the 2SLS, suggesting the IV is amplifying noise from weak first stage.

WHAT WORKED: Signs are mostly correct (5/6 negative), matching the paper's finding
that all slopes are negative and insignificant.

FIXES TO TRY FOR ATTEMPT 2:
1. Report scaled coefficients (multiply raw beta by 0.01) as the paper does
2. Try LIML estimator instead of 2SLS (more robust to weak instruments)
3. Try different sample period definitions
4. Check if using nominal NBR instead of real NBR helps
5. Try Anderson-Rubin confidence intervals for weak-instrument robust inference
