Figure 1 — "Interest-Rate Indicators of Fed Policy"

Description: A time series plot showing two interest rate series from approximately 1959 to 1990:
1. FUNDS - Federal funds rate (solid line, labeled "FUNDS")
2. FFBOND - Spread between the federal funds rate and the 10-year Treasury bond rate (dashed/lighter line, labeled "FFBOND")

Visual characteristics (from the paper's figure):
- Y-axis: "Rate (Percentage)" ranging from about -4 to 20
- X-axis: "YEAR" with labels at 62, 66, 70, 74, 78, 82, 86, 90
- FUNDS (solid line) ranges from near 0 to about 18-19 (peak around 1980-1981)
- FFBOND (dashed line) fluctuates mostly between -4 and +4, with some larger swings
- Vertical lines marked "R" indicate NBER business cycle peaks (recession starts). There appear to be 4 "R" markers, corresponding to the Romer dates: approximately 1968-69, 1974, 1978-79, and one more
- The figure shows that FUNDS dominates the movements; FFBOND is much smoother
- Both series show the dramatic rise in rates around 1979-1981 (Volcker period)

Key features to replicate:
1. Two time series on same plot
2. FUNDS as solid line
3. FFBOND as dashed or lighter line
4. Vertical lines at Romer dates (marked with "R"): approximately Dec 1968, Apr 1974, Aug 1978, Oct 1979
5. Y-axis from -4 to 20
6. X-axis from ~1959 to ~1990
7. Title: "FIGURE 1. INTEREST-RATE INDICATORS OF FED POLICY"
