Analysis of discrepancy patterns:
1. M2 is consistently OVER-estimated, M1 is consistently UNDER-estimated compared to paper.
   This suggests that our M1 and M2 data may differ from what the paper used.
   The paper likely used M1 and M2 data vintages from 1992, which have been revised.

2. CPI contribution is consistently higher than the paper. This may be due to CPI revisions.

3. Own variable contributions are generally lower than the paper.

4. FUNDS contributions tend to be lower in our results, suggesting the monetary variables
   (especially M2) are absorbing variance that should go to FUNDS.

Possible fixes to try:
1. The data vintage issue is likely the main driver. We cannot fix this directly.
2. Check if the VAR trend specification matters (constant vs. constant+trend).
3. Try using nominal variables instead of real for personal income/retail sales/consumption
   (though the paper says real).
4. Check if the paper might be using log levels for some variables where we're using levels.
5. Check statsmodels FEVD computation - the decomp array ordering may differ from expectation.
