Core issue: Data vintage effects, especially for M2.
- Our M2 data has been revised since the paper was published in 1992.
- The Federal Reserve significantly revised M2 definitions in 1980 and subsequently.
- This causes M2's contribution to be systematically different from the paper.
- CPI also shows higher contributions in our results, possibly due to CPI revisions.

- Housing starts: Very close to paper values in both panels
- Unemployment rate: Fairly close in Panel B

- BOND contributions: Generally close

What's off:

- Personal income: Own way too low (22.4 vs 48.2), M2 way too high (20.6 vs 0.1)
