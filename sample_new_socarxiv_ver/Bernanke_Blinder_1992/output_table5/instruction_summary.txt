Variable mapping:

Causal variables (X):
- U (unemployment rate) → unemp_male_2554 / 100 (convert to decimal)
- INFL (inflation rate) → Compute as change in log CPI: diff(log_cpi), which gives monthly inflation rate in decimal form

Indicator variables (Z):
- FFBOND → ffbond (= funds_rate - treasury_10y, already in dataset)
- DRBOND → Need discount rate. If not available, this is the spread between discount rate and long-term bond rate. May need to approximate or find the discount rate series.
  FRED series for discount rate: INTDSRUSM193N or similar. If unavailable, try to omit or approximate.
- Annualized real growth rate of nonborrowed reserves → 12 * diff(log_nonborrowed_reserves_real)
  (annualized monthly growth rate of real nonborrowed reserves)

Model: MIMIC model estimated by maximum likelihood
- y* = Xc + u (measurement equation linking latent variable to causes)
- Z = y*b' + v (structural equation linking indicators to latent variable)
- Normalization: set loading of FFBOND = 1
- No constant (variables as deviations from means)
- Can be estimated using statsmodels or scipy optimization

Sample: 1959-08-01 to 1979-09-01

IMPORTANT NOTE: The discount rate may need to be downloaded separately from FRED.
Try FRED series: INTDSRUSM193N (Discount Rate for United States) or MDISCRT.

Alternative approach: Since the MIMIC model is complex, an OLS regression of FFBOND on lagged U and INFL can approximate the reaction function coefficients (since FFBOND loading is normalized to 1, its projection gives similar results).

Dataset: bb1992_data.csv
