Table 5 — "Modified Avery Reaction Function, 1959:8-1979:9"

This table presents a MIMIC (Multiple Indicators, Multiple Causes) model estimating the Fed's reaction function.

The model is:
(5) y* = Xc + u  (latent policy variable as function of causal variables)
(6) Z = y*b' + v  (observed indicators as function of latent variable)

Where:
- y* is the unobserved latent monetary policy variable (T x 1)
- X is the matrix of causal variables: 6 lags of unemployment rate (U) and 6 lags of inflation rate (INFL), measured in decimals. T x 12.
- Z is the vector of observed indicators of policy: FFBOND (funds rate - 10-year bond rate), DRBOND (spread between discount rate and long-term bond rate), and annualized real growth rate of nonborrowed reserves. T x 3.
- All variables measured as deviations from means, no constant term included.

Normalization: coefficient of FFBOND in equation (6) set to unity.

Notes:
- U is the unemployment rate and INFL is the inflation rate, measured in decimals
- The table reports the effects of U and INFL on a latent indicator of monetary policy
- The chi-square statistic tests the overidentifying restrictions of the model
- Sample period: 1959:8-1979:9
- Indicator variables (Z): FFBOND, spread between discount rate and bond rate (DRBOND), annualized real nonborrowed reserves growth
- The coefficient on FFBOND in Z equation normalized to 1

The paper notes that the absolute magnitudes of coefficients are not meaningful (identified only up to a scaling factor). The PATTERN of response is what matters: increased unemployment loosens policy, increased inflation tightens it.
