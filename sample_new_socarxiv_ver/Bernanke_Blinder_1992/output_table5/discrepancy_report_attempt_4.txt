   The INFL coefficients are much better:
   INFL(-1): 6.3*0.39 = 2.5 vs 7.9  (off but sign correct)

   Actually, looking at raw values: the MD estimator gives raw coefficients
   that when scaled by optimal factor produce reasonable INFL but not U.

2. The problem: MIMIC coefficients are identified up to a single scale factor.
   But the relative pattern of U vs INFL matters. The paper shows:
   - U(-2) is the largest in magnitude (-65.9)
   - U(-1) is small (-5.0)
   - U(-5) is very small (1.4)
   Our estimates show U(-1) = -35.5 (much larger than paper's -5.0 relative to U(-2))
   and U(-5) = 19.5 (much larger than paper's 1.4 relative to U(-2)).

3. Data vintage is the most likely explanation for the U coefficient discrepancies.
   The unemployment rate has been revised multiple times since 1992.
