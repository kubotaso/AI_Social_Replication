The 14 missing PID voters are excluded. In Bartels' 1995 data, these may have had
valid PID values, or conversely some of our valid PID voters may have been coded
differently. With only 1 N difference (758 vs 759), it's a specific respondent-level
coding change that shifts the weak coefficient substantially.

For 1974, the 8-observation N difference (790 vs 798) suggests 8 voters had their PID
recoded to missing in the 2019 CDF. If those 8 were predominantly strong partisans
who voted with their party, removing them could inflate the strong coefficient (1.262
vs paper's 1.148).
