Figure 1: The Distribution of Party Identification, 1952-1996
Two panels (stacked vertically). Title: "Proportions of National Election Study Sample"
Top panel: Two lines - "Strong" Identifiers (filled circles, solid line) and "Weak" Identifiers (open circles, dashed line). Proportion of NES sample who are Strong or Weak identifiers (of either party) over time.
Bottom panel: Two lines - Independent "Leaners" (open circles, dashed line) and "Pure" Independents (filled circles, solid line).
X-axis: 1956 to 1996 (election years only - every 4 years labeled)
Y-axis: 0.0 to 0.5
Data: Compute from VCF0301 for each year 1952-1996 (even years only).
- Strong identifiers: VCF0301 in {1, 7} (Strong Dem + Strong Rep)
- Weak identifiers: VCF0301 in {2, 6} (Weak Dem + Weak Rep)
- Ind Leaners: VCF0301 in {3, 5} (Ind-Dem + Ind-Rep)
- Pure Independents: VCF0301 = 4
