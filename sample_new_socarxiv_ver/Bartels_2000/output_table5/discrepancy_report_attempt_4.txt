The fundamental problem is that the ANES Cumulative Data File (CDF) has fewer
panel respondents for 1960 than Bartels had access to. Bartels used 911 panel
respondents for 1960, but the CDF only yields 736 with the vote union approach
(VCF0707 + VCF0706).

For 1992, despite N being nearly exact (759 vs 760), the LL difference of 14.3
suggests our sample has slightly different composition. This could be due to:
- Different handling of independent/third-party leaners
- Different coding of the House vote variable
- Bartels may have had access to additional variables not in the CDF
