Figure 4: Partisan Voting in Presidential Elections, White Southerners and White Non-Southerners
Title: "Estimated Impact of Party Identification on Presidential Vote Propensity"
Two lines:
- "White Non-South" (crosses/plus markers, solid line)
- "White South" (filled circles, dashed line)
X-axis: 1956 to 1996, Y-axis: 0.0 to 2.0
Note: "Average probit coefficients, major-party voters only."

Same methodology as Figure 3 but computed separately for:
1. White non-Southern respondents (VCF0105a=1 AND VCF0113=2)
2. White Southern respondents (VCF0105a=1 AND VCF0113=1)

For each subgroup and year: run probit model on major-party presidential voters, then compute average coefficient weighted by proportions of strong/weak/leaners in that subgroup's full sample (voters + nonvoters).
