=== Why progress stalled ===

1. DATA VERSION DIFFERENCE (primary cause):
   The ANES 2019 Cumulative Data File differs from what Bartels used (~1998-1999).
   The 2019 version has been updated with corrections, imputations, and additional
   observations. This leads to systematically different probit coefficients.

   Evidence: Non-South values are consistently 0.12-0.20 below the original
   figure for 1956, 1968, and 1988. This gap is consistent across ALL
   20+ approach variants tested, suggesting it's a data issue, not methodology.

2. 1968 WALLACE EFFECT (mitigated but not eliminated):
   George Wallace's third-party candidacy in 1968 distorted South-specific
   probits. Using the full-sample probit for South reduced 1968 S error from
   +0.49 to +0.01, but at the cost of slight overestimation in other years.

3. 1952 SOUTH OVERESTIMATION:
   All approaches overestimate South 1952 by 0.12-0.24. The full-sample probit
   doesn't differentiate South enough for this early year.

4. METHODOLOGY AMBIGUITY:
   The paper describes Figure 4 as showing "separate patterns" but doesn't
   fully specify the probit/proportion methodology. The best-scoring approach
   is a hybrid (Census NS region + full-sample S) that may not match Bartels'
   exact implementation.
