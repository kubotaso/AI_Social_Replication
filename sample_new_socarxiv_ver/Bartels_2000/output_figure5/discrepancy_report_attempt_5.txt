KEY FINDING:
The main source of difference between our figure and the original is NOT
the probit coefficients (which are extremely close) but the proportions.
Bartels used the 1948-1994 CD-ROM Cumulative Data File (May 1995)
supplemented with specific panel data files and the 1996 study. Our modern
cumulative file has slightly different case counts and thus slightly different
proportions (e.g., 1952: ours .375/.398/.178 vs Bartels' .391/.376/.176).
This leads to systematically slightly different average coefficients,
especially in years where the "strong" coefficient is large (since the strong
proportion difference is the most noticeable).
