Figure 5: Partisan Voting in Presidential and Congressional Elections
Title: "Estimated Impact of Party Identification on Presidential and Congressional Vote Propensities"
Two lines:
- "Congress" (crosses/plus, dashed line) - congressional elections
- "President" (filled circles, solid line) - presidential elections
X-axis: 1956 to 1996, Y-axis: 0.0 to 2.0
Note: "Average probit coefficients, major-party voters only."

Presidential line = same as Figure 3 (from Table 1 results).
Congressional line = same methodology but using Table 2 results (congressional vote probit coefficients weighted by proportions).

For congressional elections, the average coefficient is computed the same way: coef_strong*prop_strong + coef_weak*prop_weak + coef_leaners*prop_leaners, where proportions come from the full sample (all respondents with valid party ID) in each election year.
