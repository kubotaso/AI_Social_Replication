Figure 6: Partisan Voting in Congressional Elections, Controlling for Incumbency
Title: "Estimated Impact of Party Identification on Congressional Vote Propensity"
Two lines:
- "Without Incumbency" (open circles, dashed line) - from Table 2
- "With Incumbency" (filled circles, solid line) - from Table 3
X-axis: 1956 to 1996, Y-axis: 0.0 to 2.0
Note: "Average probit coefficients, major-party voters only."

Compares average probit coefficients from Table 2 (without incumbency) vs Table 3 (with incumbency) for the years where both overlap (1970-1996, excluding 1972).

The paper says: "Figure 6 compares estimated levels of partisan voting from the analyses in Table 2 (without incumbency) and Table 3 (with incumbency) for the period where the two analyses overlap."

For years only in Table 2, the "Without Incumbency" line covers all years 1952-1996.
The "With Incumbency" line covers only 1970, 1974-1996.
